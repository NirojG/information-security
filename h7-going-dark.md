## x) Quintin 2014: 7 Things You Should Know About Tor (Summary)
- NSA leaks shows that Tor still works, and Tor has not been completely broken in the cryptographical level. 
- Most successful attacks on Tor happen through various kinds of browser exploits, and user mistakes, or the traffic.
- Tor is used by many kinds of people and groups such as activists, journalists, families, researchers, and the military.
- Using Tor does not mean someone is helping criminals.
- Tor does not contain a military backdoor.
- The software is open-source and has been reviewed by security experts.
- No one in the U.S. has been prosecuted simply for running a Tor relay.
- Tor is easy to use through tools like Tor Browser or Tails.
- Tor is slower than normal internet but performance has improved.
- Tor is not perfect and users can lose anonymity if they use it incorrectly.

## x) Shavers & Bair 2016: Hiding Behind the Keyboard: The Tor Browser (Summary)

- Tor is a modified version of the Firefox browser designed to hide a user’s real IP address.
- By hiding the IP address, it becomes very difficult to trace or identify the user.
- Tor is easy to use and does not require technical knowledge.
- Anyone with internet access can download Tor and browse anonymously.
- Tor was created to support anonymous and unrestricted communication online.
- It helps people access blocked websites and communicate privately.
- Like any technology, Tor can also be misused for criminal activities.
- Tor was originally developed by the U.S. government in 2002.
- Today it is open-source and not controlled by any single organization.
- Privacy and security experts worldwide help improve and maintain it.
- Tor traffic passes through three relays: entry, middle, and exit.
- The route changes about every 10 minutes, making tracking very difficult.
- Each relay only knows the previous relay, not the full path.
- Tor works like sending a letter through multiple people to hide the sender.
- Each layer of encryption is removed step-by-step, like peeling an onion.
- The exit node sends the final data to the destination.
- The destination only sees the exit node’s IP address, not the original user.
- Running a Tor exit node can cause misunderstandings because activity appears to come from that IP.
