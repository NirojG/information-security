## x) Quintin 2014: 7 Things You Should Know About Tor (Summary)
- In this article we can get insights on how NSA leaks shows that Tor still works, and Tor has not been completely broken in the cryptographical level. 
- Most successful attacks that happens on Tor happen through various kinds of browser exploits, and user mistakes, and traffic etc.
- Tor is used by many kinds of people and groups such as activists, journalists, researchers, enthusiasts, military etc.
- Using Tor does not mean its a crime, Tor is usually used for privacy and anonymity.
- Tor does not contain any backdoors, such as military backdoor.
- The Tor browser software is an open-source and it has been reviewed by various professionals and security experts.
- So, in the article its stated that No one in the U.S. has been prosecuted just for running a Tor relay.
- Tor is slower than normal internet that we use in our everyday basis but its performance has improved over time.
- As per any softwares, Tor is not perfect, it has issues and the users can also lose their anonymity if they use Tor incorrectly.

## x) Shavers & Bair 2016: Hiding Behind the Keyboard: The Tor Browser (Summary)

- In this article we get more insights on Tor, Tor (the onion router) is a modified version of the Firefox browser which is especially designed to hide a user’s real IP address for becoming private and anonymous.
- By hiding the IP address via Tor browser, it becomes very difficult for anyone to trace or identify the user.
- Tor browser is usually easy to use for beginners and it also does not need any prior technical knowledge.
- Anyone with the internet access can easily download Tor and start browsing anonymously.
- Tor browser helps people access blocked websites and communicate privately.
- Like any other technology currently developed, Tor browser also can be misused for various criminal activities.
- Tor was originally developed by the U.S. government in 2002.
- Today it is an open-source software and which is not controlled by any single organization.
- Privacy and security experts and professionals worldwide help improve and maintain Tor.
- Tor traffic passes through three relays: entry, middle, and exit.
- The route of Tor changes about every 10 minutes, and the tracking of routes of Tor is extremely difficult.
- Each relay on Tor, only knows the previous relay it doesn't know the full path.
- How the Tor works is, it is like sending a letter through multiple people to hide the sender details.
- Each layer of encryption is removed step-by-step, like peeling an onion.
- The exit node of Tor sends the final data to the destination and the destination only sees the exit node’s IP address, not the original user Ip address therefore making everything anonymous.
